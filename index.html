Biography

Sarojini Naidu was an Indian independence activist, poet, and politician. She was born on February 13, 1879, in Hyderabad, India, and passed away on March 2, 1949. Naidu was an important figure in India's struggle for independence from British rule and was known for her work as a poet and orator.

Political Career

Naidu was a significant figure in the Indian National Movement. She joined the Indian National Congress and participated in the freedom struggle. She was the first woman to become the president of the Indian National Congress and later became the first woman to serve as a governor in independent India.

Early Influence and Inspiration

Her father, Aghorenath Chattopadhyay, was a scientist and educator, while her mother, Barada Sundari Devi, was a poet. Sarojini's early education and exposure to literature played a crucial role in shaping her literary and political career.

Writing Career

Sarojini Naidu's poetry was rich with themes of Indian culture, women's emancipation, and nationalism. She was known as the 'Nightingale of India' for her lyrical, rhythmic poetry.

The Bird of Time

'The Bird of Time: Songs of Life, Death & the Spring' is one of Sarojini Naidu's most celebrated works, published in 1912. It features a collection of poems that reflect her profound thoughts and emotions.

The Broken Wing

'The Broken Wing: Songs of Love, Death and Destiny' is another renowned collection of poems by Sarojini Naidu, published in 1917. This work further established her as a leading poet of her time.
